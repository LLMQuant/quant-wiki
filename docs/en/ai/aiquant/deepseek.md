# DeepSeek: The Quant-Backed Startup That Rattled US Tech Stocks

![](https://fastly.jsdelivr.net/gh/bucketio/img9@main/2024/10/20/1729465031968-b3c8959e-1d37-4b8a-91b1-b0b0dfe25143.png)

**DeepSeek**, a Chinese AI research lab founded in **2023** by **Liang Wenfeng**, has sent shockwaves through the global technology landscape with its open-source **DeepSeek-R1** model, mounting a serious challenge to the AI dominance of American tech giants. Here is a closer look at what propelled DeepSeek into the global spotlight and why it triggered such massive capital market volatility.

## At Time of Publication: US Tech Stocks Plunge

- **Bloomberg** reported that Nasdaq 100 futures and the European Stoxx 600 Technology sub-index both fell sharply, erasing a combined **$1.2 trillion** in market capitalization;
- The **"Magnificent Seven"** took heavy hits:

  - Nvidia: **-11%**
  - Alphabet (GOOG/GOOGL): -3%
  - Amazon (AMZN): -2.4%
  - Meta (META): -1.4%
  - Microsoft (MSFT): -3.2%
  - Tesla (TSLA): -0.61%
  - Apple (AAPL) bucked the trend with a +1.2% gain, driven by surging downloads of DeepSeek-related apps on the App Store

- Chip stocks: ASML -6.2%, Broadcom (AVGO) -12%, Qualcomm (QCOM) -1.4%, AMD -4.5%

DeepSeek's "low hardware requirements" philosophy clearly put pressure on chip manufacturers and cloud computing providers.

![](https://fastly.jsdelivr.net/gh/bucketio/img3@main/2025/01/27/1737993303294-95ce00b6-d9ec-48cd-a36d-45f56b88dc89.png)

## DeepSeek's Origins: Two Key Dimensions

1. **Founder: Liang Wenfeng**
   - In addition to founding DeepSeek, Liang also established **High-Flyer**, the quantitative hedge fund that backs DeepSeek. The lab was born with a dual "finance + technology" DNA from day one.
2. **Initial breakthrough: DeepSeek V3**
   - In **December 2024**, DeepSeek released its first open-source LLM -- DeepSeek V3, reportedly trained for under **$6 million** using Nvidia **H800** GPUs;
   - Its "high performance, low cost" proposition quickly attracted attention across the global AI community.

---

## DeepSeek-R1: A New Benchmark for Low-Cost, High-Performance AI

Building on **V3**, DeepSeek's **R1** model is widely seen as its flagship challenge to OpenAI.

### 1. Reinforcement Learning First: Moving Beyond Conventional SFT
- DeepSeek-R1 relies almost entirely on **reinforcement learning (RL)** to iterate the model, dramatically reducing dependence on human-annotated data;
- RL enables the model to independently explore sophisticated reasoning patterns: **self-verification**, **reflection**, and **Chain-of-Thought (CoT)** among other advanced "thinking" capabilities.

### 2. Performance: Rivaling OpenAI's GPT-o1 at 95% Lower Cost
> **"DeepSeek-R1 approaches OpenAI's o1 on math, coding, and reasoning, yet costs roughly 5% as much!"**
-- Roth Capital research report

- In the AIME 2024 mathematics competition, DeepSeek-R1 achieved a **pass@1** accuracy of **71%**, rising to **86.7%** with majority voting;
- On the App Store, DeepSeek-related applications rapidly climbed to the top of the charts, with users praising the model's transparency, lightweight design, and ease of use.

![](https://fastly.jsdelivr.net/gh/bucketio/img12@main/2025/01/27/1737993343153-307f4aeb-0b73-4021-9318-1c1a31d1dd74.png)

### 3. Open-Source Model, Partially Open Data
- **MIT License**: Researchers and developers can freely reuse, modify, and extend the model;
- Training data is not fully released, limiting complete reproducibility. Nonetheless, DeepSeek has provided the global AI community with valuable technical insights and methodological references.

![Yann LeCun's comments on DeepSeek's open-source release](https://fastly.jsdelivr.net/gh/bucketio/img9@main/2025/01/27/1737993365857-c54e1984-37b2-45e4-92d6-586c22818625.png)

## Reshaping the AI Landscape: High-End AI on Low-End Hardware

DeepSeek's most disruptive technical achievement is proving that cutting-edge AI no longer requires the most expensive **Nvidia A100** or **H100** GPUs. DeepSeek-V3 was trained primarily on **H800** chips for under **$6 million** -- a fraction of what many US counterparts spend (often hundreds of millions of dollars).

> **"This means that companies whose premium valuations are built on the assumption of expensive AI hardware may need to reassess their outlook."**

## Broader Implications: America's AI Reinvestment

Shortly after DeepSeek-R1's release, **OpenAI CEO Sam Altman** met with **US President Donald Trump** to announce the **Stargate Project**, a **$500 billion**, four-year AI infrastructure initiative aimed at maintaining US global leadership in AI hardware.

Yet DeepSeek's emergence suggests that spending lavishly on top-tier compute alone may not be enough to maintain a decisive lead.

---

## How DeepSeek Views Its Own Position

1. **Reducing the cost of AI**
   - Core philosophy: Leverage reinforcement learning and open-source collaboration to give research institutions and companies low-barrier access to high-performance AI deployment.
2. **Narrowing the US-China AI gap**
   - DeepSeek is widely regarded as a symbol of Chinese AI teams competing at the frontier alongside US counterparts such as OpenAI and Meta.
3. **Diversified business model**
   - From hedge fund backing to consumer-facing App Store products, DeepSeek is positioned for rapid expansion along multiple paths.

## Looking Ahead: Is DeepSeek Just the Beginning?

> DeepSeek went from its **2023** founding to making global headlines in **2025**, accomplishing in roughly two years what most observers thought impossible.

- **For the AI industry**: It represents a new paradigm where "less hardware can still produce frontier results," potentially triggering a reallocation of resources;
- **For capital markets**: The massive market cap swings reflect investors reassessing the future AI landscape;
- **For global AI research**: The open-source model with partially withheld data illustrates DeepSeek's balancing act between advancing research and protecting competitive advantage.

Regardless of what happens next, DeepSeek-R1 has dropped a bombshell on the global AI community. As initiatives like the Stargate Project move forward, the race between compute and algorithmic efficiency will only intensify. Which direction will define the next phase? Time will tell.
