# What Is a News Trader?
![](https://fastly.jsdelivr.net/gh/bucketio/img9@main/2024/10/20/1729465031968-b3c8959e-1d37-4b8a-91b1-b0b0dfe25143.png)

A news trader is a trader or investor who makes decisions based on news announcements. Breaking news, economic reports, and other reported events can have a short-lived effect on the price action of stocks, bonds, and other securities. News traders seek to profit by capitalizing on market sentiment leading up to the release of significant news and by trading the market's reaction after the news is published.

### Key Takeaways

- News traders trade on scheduled announcements, aiming to profit from short-term volatility.
- News traders may also trade significant unplanned events that affect the domestic or global economy.
- News traders tend to hold positions for a very short time, as the impact of news typically fades quickly after release.

## Understanding News Traders

The adage "buy the rumor, sell the news" recognizes that rumors have one effect on a security's price while news can have the opposite effect. For this reason, news traders focus on trading in the period just before or shortly after a news release, when the market is still reacting. These periods are typically characterized by elevated volatility, creating opportunities for profit.

News traders primarily try to profit from the timing or likely content of scheduled news announcements. When the news is scheduled -- such as an earnings release or a Federal Reserve meeting -- news trading is more about betting on the probable significance of the announcement. In practice, the Federal Reserve tries to soften the market impact of its statements by signaling major policy decisions well in advance, yet even these policy signals have become tradable events.

When news surprises everyone -- such as a natural disaster or a black swan event -- news traders attempt to position themselves for profit. Sometimes this means trading the volatility itself, or making a judgment call on the immediate directional impact of the news on current price trends.

**Note:** In most cases, a news trader is a type of day trader, since they generally open and close trades within the same day.

## News Trader Tools and Strategies

News traders leverage multiple strategies with a focus on market psychology and historical data. Traders may examine historical data -- for example, past earnings reports -- to predict how upcoming news (such as a forthcoming earnings report) might affect prices. By becoming familiar with specific markets, news traders can make educated guesses about whether a security's price will rise or fall following a news release.

News traders can also set up queries and alerts to gather breaking news and correlate it with price changes on a chart. If certain criteria are met, the news trader enters a bullish or bearish position based on their trading strategy. Because news is timely and typically short-lived in its impact, the profit opportunity exists only while the news is fresh.

A popular strategy among news traders is known as "fading," which involves trading against the prevailing sentiment once market enthusiasm wanes. For example, a stock might open sharply higher in pre-market trading on a positive earnings announcement. A news trader might watch for this optimism to peak and then short the stock during the session. Even though the stock may still be up significantly from the prior day, the trader may have profited from the intraday high-to-low swing.
