# What Is a Base Year?

![](https://fastly.jsdelivr.net/gh/bucketio/img9@main/2024/10/20/1729465031968-b3c8959e-1d37-4b8a-91b1-b0b0dfe25143.png)

A base year is the first in a series of years in an economic or financial index. In this context, it is typically set to an arbitrary level of 100. Periodically updated base years are introduced to keep data in a given index current. A base year is also used to measure the growth of a company. Any year can serve as a base year, but analysts typically choose recent years.

### Key Takeaways

- A base year is the first in a series of years in an economic or financial index.
- A base year is also used to measure business activity, such as sales growth from one period to the next.
- Any year can serve as a base year; the choice depends on the analysis being conducted.

## Understanding Base Years

A base year is used for comparison in measuring business activity or an economic or financial index. For example, to find the rate of inflation between 2016 and 2024, 2016 is the base year -- the first year in the time series. A base year can also describe the starting point from which growth is measured, or serve as a baseline for calculating same-store sales.

Many financial ratios are growth-based because analysts want to understand how much a particular figure changes from one period to the next.

The growth rate formula is: (Current Year - Base Year) / Base Year.

In ratio analysis, the past period serves as the base.

Growth analysis is a commonly used way to describe company performance, particularly in sales. If Company A grows sales from $100,000 to $140,000, this implies 40% sales growth, where $100,000 represents the base year value.

**Note:** Investors can perform base year analysis on a company's financial statements to determine whether its bottom line is growing consistently.

## Base Year and Horizontal Analysis

Horizontal analysis involves comparing financial data across multiple periods to track changes and trends. The base year serves as the reference point or benchmark for these comparisons, providing a baseline against which current and future financial data are measured.

When performing year-over-year horizontal analysis, base year financial figures are used to calculate percentage changes in key metrics -- such as revenue, net income, or expenses -- in subsequent years. For example, if the base year is 2022 and the company's revenue that year was $1 million, then revenue figures for 2023 and beyond would be compared against this base to determine growth or decline. Horizontal analysis is relatively straightforward, especially for publicly traded companies that must publish these year-over-year figures each quarter.

## Base Year and Same-Store Sales Calculations

Companies are always looking for ways to increase sales. One way to grow sales is to open new stores or branches. New stores naturally have higher growth rates because they are starting from zero, and every new-store sale is an incremental sale. As a result, analysts focus on additional factors, such as how much sales grew on a same-store basis. This is also known as measuring comparable stores or comp-store sales.

In calculating same-store sales, the base year represents the starting point for the number of stores and the sales those stores generated. If Company A has 100 stores that together sold $100,000 last year, each store sold an average of $1,000. This is the base year. Following this method, the base year establishes the baseline sales and baseline number of stores.

Suppose Company A opens another 100 stores the following year and these stores generate $50,000, while same-store sales decline 10%, from $100,000 to $90,000. The company can report a 40% increase in sales -- from $100,000 to $140,000 -- but savvy analysts are more interested in the 10% decline in same-store sales.

## Base Year and Real Estate Leases

The concept of a base year is also found in real estate. In commercial real estate, the base year is used to determine a tenant's responsibility for certain operating expenses over the term of a lease. The base year typically refers to the first full year of the lease. Expenses during this period serve as a benchmark or floor for calculating any expense increases the tenant may be responsible for in the future.

For example, if the lease base year is set as 2024 and operating expenses that year are $100,000, then in subsequent years the tenant would be responsible for their share of any expenses exceeding $100,000. If expenses rise to $110,000 in 2025, the tenant would pay their proportionate share of the $10,000 increase.

The base year also relates to rent escalations. In commercial real estate, long-term leases can span 10 years with annual increases of, say, 4%. If the base year rent is $1 million per year, each subsequent year's rent will be 4% higher than the prior year (escalating from the $1 million base). The first year after the base year would be $1,040,000.

## How Is a Base Year Used?

A base year is used for comparison or measurement of business activity, or to track economic or financial indexes. For example, the base year is used in same-store sales calculations. It is also used in GDP calculations.

## How Do You Choose a Base Year?

The choice of a base year depends on the analysis being conducted. For example, a company founded in 2021 might use that year as a base year against which to measure future sales growth.

## How Do You Calculate a Growth Rate?

A growth rate is calculated by dividing the difference between the ending value and the beginning value of the analysis period by the beginning value. The growth rate formula is: (Current Year - Base Year) / Base Year. The base year represents the starting point from which growth is judged.

## Conclusion

The base year is used in economic and financial indexes as well as to measure company growth. The choice of base year depends on the analysis being conducted. When researching stocks, investors can perform base year analysis to track a company's growth trajectory or to inform investment decisions.
