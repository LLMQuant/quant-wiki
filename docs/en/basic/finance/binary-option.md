# What Are Binary Options?
![](https://fastly.jsdelivr.net/gh/bucketio/img9@main/2024/10/20/1729465031968-b3c8959e-1d37-4b8a-91b1-b0b0dfe25143.png)

Binary options are financial derivatives that allow investors to place all-or-nothing bets on events and asset price movements. Their popularity has surged in recent years. However, critics -- including U.S. regulators who have long warned investors -- note that these instruments resemble gambling more than investing and pose significant risks of fraud and loss for unsuspecting consumers.

The outcome of these options is based on a "yes or no" proposition, hence the name "binary." If a binary option expires in the money, the trader receives a payout; if it expires out of the money, the trader loses the investment. In 2008, the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) approved the listing of binary options on exchanges. Since then, the Chicago Board Options Exchange and the North American Derivatives Exchange (Nadex) have offered standardized binary options to retail traders.

Despite the availability of regulated exchange-traded binary options, the majority of binary options trading in the United States still takes place through unregulated offshore platforms, leading to widespread fraud. [1] Binary options are banned in many jurisdictions around the world, including Australia, Canada, Europe, and the United Kingdom. [3]

### Key Takeaways

- Binary options are based on a "yes or no" outcome.
- If a binary option expires in the money, the trader receives a payout; if it expires out of the money, the trader loses the investment.
- Binary options have a fixed payout and a fixed maximum loss.
- Binary options do not give the trader a position in the underlying asset.
- The majority of binary options trading occurs on platforms outside the United States.

## How Binary Options Work

A binary option has an expiration date and time. At expiration, the price of the underlying asset or event must be on the correct side of the strike price set by the exchange for the trader to profit.

Binary options exercise automatically, meaning the gain or loss on the trade is automatically credited or debited to the trader's account upon expiration. This means the buyer of a binary option either receives a payout or loses the entire investment -- there is no middle ground.

For example, suppose the proposition is that ABC's stock price will be above $25 at 10:45 a.m. on April 22, 2021. A trader who believes the price will be above $25 at that date and time is willing to stake $100 on the trade. If ABC is trading above $25 at that time, the trader receives a payout based on the agreed terms. For instance, if the payout ratio is 70%, the binary options broker credits $70 to the trader's account. If the stock is below $25 at that date and time, the trader loses the $100 investment.

Many people find binary options easier to understand than conventional options, which is one reason for their popularity. Because of the inherent risk, trade sizes are typically capped -- for example, Nadex limits positions to approximately $2,500. [5]

## Binary Options vs. Vanilla Options

### Binary Options

- **Ownership potential:** No ownership of the underlying asset.
- **Risk and payout:** Fixed maximum payout; loss limited to the amount invested.
- **Regulation:** Often traded on unregulated platforms, increasing fraud risk.

### Vanilla Options

- **Ownership potential:** Potential ownership of the underlying asset.
- **Risk and payout:** Fixed risk, but profits vary with the underlying asset's price movement.
- **Regulation:** Traded on regulated U.S. exchanges, subject to U.S. market regulations.

Standard American-style options give the holder the right to buy or sell an underlying asset at a specified price on or before the option's expiration date. European-style options work the same way, except the holder may only exercise on the expiration date itself. Vanilla options give the buyer potential ownership of the underlying asset. When purchasing these options, traders have a predefined risk, but profits depend on how far the underlying asset's price moves.

Binary options differ in that they do not provide the possibility of taking a position in the underlying asset. In this respect, they are purely speculative. Binary options typically specify a fixed maximum payout, while the maximum risk is limited to the amount invested in the option. The underlying "asset" can be a price or an event. Some binary options can be closed before expiration, although this typically reduces the payout (if the option is in the money).

Binary options are occasionally traded on platforms regulated by the SEC and other agencies, but most binary options trading occurs outside the United States and is not subject to U.S. regulatory oversight. Unregulated binary options brokers are not required to meet any particular standards. Consequently, investors should be highly vigilant about potential fraud. By contrast, vanilla options trade on regulated U.S. exchanges and are subject to U.S. options market regulations. [6]

## How to Trade Binary Options

When purchasing binary options, follow these steps:

- **Understand the product:** Master the basic concepts, mechanics, and factors that determine binary option payouts.
- **Research brokers:** Find a reputable binary options broker. For U.S. traders, verifying SEC regulation is essential. [7] Otherwise, your trades may be illegal. [8] If you are outside the U.S., check local regulations -- many countries ban the practice, leaving you exposed to fraudulent platforms that use various tactics to keep you depositing money. [9] Avoid platforms that claim binary options trading is "low risk," offer free "bonuses" for signing up, or encourage continued trading after losses. Check with the CFTC and other resources. Do not assume a platform is legitimate just because it has educational pages or testimonials.
- **Set up a demo account:** Many brokers offer demo accounts where traders can practice with paper or virtual money. While generally considered a risk-free way to familiarize yourself with the platform, some platforms request contact and financial information, and certain offshore operators have used such data for identity theft. [10]
- **Develop a trading strategy:** Decide which markets to trade, how much to invest, expiration times, and the types of binary options contracts to use.
- **Open a live account:** Submit additional personal information, proof of identity, and proof of residence.
- **Fund the account:** Brokers typically require a minimum deposit to begin trading. The amount varies by broker.
- **Begin trading:** Start purchasing contracts.
- **Monitor and adjust:** Continuously monitor your trades and market conditions, and be prepared to adjust your strategy as conditions change.

When trading binary options, be aware of the potential for fraud. Many binary options platforms operate outside regulated financial markets. This lack of oversight can lead to heightened fraud risk. [11] If fraud occurs, recourse is generally very limited.

Thorough research, verification of a broker's regulatory status, and a clear understanding of the risks and potential for fraud are essential before committing capital to binary options.

## Binary Options Example

As an example, entry prices on binary options represent the potential profit or loss, with all contracts settling at $100 or $0.

Suppose Colgate-Palmolive (CL) stock is trading at $64.75. A binary option has a strike price of $65 and expires tomorrow at noon. A trader can purchase the option for $40. If the stock price finishes above $65 at expiration, the option expires in the money and is worth $100. The trader earns a profit of $60 ($100 - $40).

If the option expires with Colgate's price below $65 (out of the money), the trader loses the $40 invested. The combined potential profit and loss in a binary option always totals $100.

If a trader wishes to make a larger investment, the number of contracts can be adjusted. For example, selecting three contracts raises the risk to $120 and increases the profit potential to $180.

## What Is Needed to Open a Binary Options Trading Account?

This typically includes selecting a broker, submitting an application, completing know-your-customer (KYC) verification forms, funding the account, and verifying your identity for the broker's records. [4]

## Are Binary Options Considered High Risk?

Binary options are considered high risk due to their all-or-nothing payout structure, short-term speculative nature, volatility, and the leverage involved.

Approach binary options with caution, fully understand the associated risks, and develop a trading strategy that includes risk management measures. Using a regulated and reputable broker is critical to mitigating these risks.

## When Is the Best Time to Trade Binary Options?

This depends largely on market conditions and the specific event or asset involved. The best times to trade are typically related to the product's volatility, economic events, data releases, and market liquidity. [4]

## Conclusion

Binary options are financial derivatives based on the outcome of a "yes or no" proposition, providing traders with either a fixed payout or no payout at all. Traders bet on whether the price of an underlying asset will be above or below a certain level at a specified time, leading to one of two clear outcomes: a predetermined gain or a total loss of the amount invested.

When the underlying asset is a financial instrument, traders participate in the market in a unique way. However, binary options carry significant risks, primarily due to their all-or-nothing nature and the prevalence of unregulated platforms. Before committing capital through binary options, it is advisable to thoroughly understand market conditions, develop a sound trading strategy, and clearly recognize the associated risks.
