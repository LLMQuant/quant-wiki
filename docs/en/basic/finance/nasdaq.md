# What Is the Nasdaq?

![](https://fastly.jsdelivr.net/gh/bucketio/img9@main/2024/10/20/1729465031968-b3c8959e-1d37-4b8a-91b1-b0b0dfe25143.png)

The Nasdaq is a global electronic marketplace for buying and selling securities. Its name was originally an acronym for "National Association of Securities Dealers Automated Quotations." The Nasdaq started as a subsidiary of the National Association of Securities Dealers (NASD), now known as the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority (FINRA).

The Nasdaq was launched after the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) urged the NASD to automate the market for securities not listed on an exchange. The result was the first electronic trading system. The Nasdaq began operations in 1971. [1]

### Key Takeaways

- The Nasdaq is a global online marketplace for buying and selling securities and the world's first electronic exchange.
- It operates 29 markets, one clearinghouse, and five central securities depositories in the United States and Europe.
- Most of the world's technology giants are listed on the Nasdaq.

## Understanding the Nasdaq

As noted above, the Nasdaq is a stock exchange headquartered in New York City. It began as a subsidiary of the NASD and officially commenced operations on February 8, 1971. It was the world's first automated securities exchange. [1]

The Nasdaq formally separated from the NASD in 2006 and began operating as a national securities exchange. In 2008, it merged with the Scandinavian exchange group OMX to become the Nasdaq OMX Group. [2] The company changed its name to Nasdaq, Inc. in 2015. [3]

More than 5,000 companies are listed and trade on the exchange daily. Many of these are major technology companies such as Apple (AAPL) and Microsoft (MSFT).

Since 2006, the Nasdaq has been organized into three tiers: the Nasdaq Global Select Market, the Nasdaq Global Market, and the Nasdaq Capital Market. [4]

The exchange operates 29 markets supporting the trading of equities, derivatives, fixed income, and commodities across the United States, Canada, Scandinavia, and the Baltics. The company also runs one clearinghouse and five central securities depositories in the United States and Europe. [5] Its trading technology is used by 100 exchanges in 50 countries.

## The Nasdaq Composite Index

The term "Nasdaq" is also used to refer to the Nasdaq Composite, an index comprising more than 2,500 stocks listed on the Nasdaq exchange. The index includes some of the world's largest technology companies, such as:

- Apple (AAPL)
- Microsoft (MSFT)
- Alphabet (GOOG, GOOGL)
- Meta Platforms (META)
- Amazon (AMZN)
- Tesla (TSLA) [6]

The Nasdaq Composite closed at a record high of 16,057.44 on November 19, 2021. From that point, the index declined by more than 23% through April 2022. [7] The Nasdaq Composite dropped 13.3% in April 2022, its worst monthly decline since October 2008, when the index fell 17.4% amid the global financial crisis. It closed at 13,787.92 on June 30, 2023. [8]

**Important:** Nasdaq, Inc. is listed on the Nasdaq stock market under the ticker symbol NDAQ and has been a component of the S&P 500 index since 2008.

## The Nasdaq Trading System

The Nasdaq's computerized trading system was originally designed as an alternative to the inefficient specialist system, which had been the prevailing model for nearly a century. Rapid technological evolution has made the Nasdaq's electronic trading model the standard for markets worldwide.

It was only fitting for the world's up-and-coming technology companies to list on an exchange that used the latest technology. As the technology sector rose to prominence during the 1980s and 1990s, the Nasdaq Composite became its most widely cited proxy.

This made the Nasdaq Composite the index of the dot-com boom and bust. After rising nearly 150% in the 16 months through March 2000, the Nasdaq Composite subsequently plunged nearly 80% by October 2002.

## History of the Nasdaq

In November 2016, Chief Operating Officer (COO) Adena Friedman was promoted to Chief Executive Officer (CEO), becoming the first woman to run a major U.S. exchange. [9]

On December 1, 2020, the Nasdaq proposed a new rule requiring companies listed on the exchange to report on the diversity of their boards. The rule required companies to include at least one female director and one director who is a member of an underrepresented minority or LGBTQ+, or publicly explain why they have not done so. The SEC approved the board diversity disclosure rule on August 6, 2021. [10][11]

The Nasdaq's revenue comes from clients including financial institutions, brokers, institutional investors, and corporations. Revenue is primarily derived from fees charged for:

- Market services, which give investors access to various markets
- Investment intelligence, including data, indices, and investment analytics for financial institutions, brokers, and asset managers
- Market technology, including trading and settlement platforms as well as technology safeguards against financial crime
- Corporate services, such as listing fees and investor relations services

Nasdaq reported total revenue of $6.23 billion for fiscal year 2022, ending December 31, 2022, with net income of $1.1 billion. Net revenue after deducting transaction-based expenses was $3.58 billion. The company also raised its quarterly common stock dividend to $0.78 per share in 2022, up from $0.70 in 2021. [12]

## Is the Nasdaq a U.S. Stock Market?

The Nasdaq is a U.S. securities exchange based in New York City. It officially began operations in February 1971. More than 5,000 companies -- both domestic and international -- trade on the exchange. It was the world's first automated exchange. [1]

## What Is the Nasdaq 100 Index?

The Nasdaq 100 is a large-cap growth index composed of 100 of the largest U.S. and international non-financial companies listed on the Nasdaq stock exchange, ranked by market capitalization. Major listed companies include Apple, Dollar Tree, Keurig, Sirius XM Holdings, and Zoom Video Communications. Investors interested in gaining exposure to the index's returns can do so through mutual funds, exchange-traded funds (ETFs), options, futures, and annuities that track and seek to replicate its performance. [13][14]

## Does Apple Trade on the NYSE or the Nasdaq?

Apple trades on the Nasdaq under the ticker symbol AAPL. The company went public on December 12, 1980, with an initial trading price of $22. [15] Apple stock closed at $190.25 on June 30, 2023.

## Conclusion

The Nasdaq is one of the major securities exchanges in the United States. More than 5,000 domestic and international companies are listed, with a strong focus on the technology sector. The exchange began operations in 1971 and was the world's first automated exchange. The Nasdaq Composite, comprising more than 2,500 listed companies, is one of the most closely watched stock market indices in the world and is considered a barometer of the U.S. and global economy.
