# What Is Unlevered Beta?

![](https://fastly.jsdelivr.net/gh/bucketio/img9@main/2024/10/20/1729465031968-b3c8959e-1d37-4b8a-91b1-b0b0dfe25143.png)

Beta is a measure of market risk. Unlevered beta (also known as asset beta) measures the market risk of a company without the impact of debt.

"Unlevering" beta strips out the financial effects of leverage, thereby isolating the risk attributable solely to the company's assets. In other words, it quantifies how much the company's equity contributes to its risk profile.

### Key Takeaways

- Levered beta (commonly referred to simply as beta, or equity beta) is a measure of market risk. It accounts for both debt and equity when assessing a company's risk profile.
- Unlevered beta removes the debt component to isolate the risk attributable solely to the company's assets.
- A high debt-to-equity ratio generally corresponds to increased risk associated with a company's stock.
- A beta of 1 indicates the stock carries the same level of risk as the market, while betas above or below 1 reflect risk thresholds higher or lower than the market, respectively.

## Understanding Unlevered Beta

Beta is the slope coefficient of a stock regressed against a benchmark market index such as the S&P 500. A key determinant of beta is leverage, which measures the ratio of a company's debt to equity. Levered beta measures the risk of a firm with both debt and equity in its capital structure relative to market volatility. The alternative measure is unlevered beta.

Unlevering beta eliminates any beneficial or detrimental effects gained by adding debt to the firm's capital structure. Comparing companies' unlevered betas gives investors a clearer picture of the risk composition they are assuming when purchasing the stock.

$$ \text{Unlevered Beta (Asset Beta)} =\frac{ \text{Levered Beta (Equity Beta)} } {\left( 1 + \frac{\left( 1-\text{Tax Rate} \right )\times\text{Debt}}{\text{Equity}} \right )} $$

Consider a company that increases its debt, thereby raising its debt-to-equity ratio. This will cause a larger proportion of earnings to be used to service that debt, amplifying investor uncertainty about future earnings streams. Consequently, the company's stock is perceived as increasingly risky -- but this additional risk does not originate from market risk.

**Tip:** Isolating and removing the debt component from the overall risk yields the unlevered beta.

The level of debt a company carries affects its performance and makes it more sensitive to changes in its share price. Note that although the company being analyzed carries debt on its financial statements, unlevered beta treats it as if it had no debt, stripping out all debt from the calculation. Because companies have different capital structures and debt levels, an analyst can calculate unlevered beta to compare them effectively against one another or against the market. In this way, only the sensitivity of a firm's assets (equity) to the market is taken into account.

To unlever beta, one must know the company's levered beta as well as its debt-to-equity ratio and corporate tax rate.

## Systematic Risk and Beta

Systematic risk is the type of risk caused by factors beyond a company's control. It cannot be diversified away. Examples include natural disasters, political elections, inflation, and wars. Beta is used to measure the level of systematic risk, or volatility, of a stock or portfolio.

Beta is a statistical measure that compares the volatility of a stock's price to the volatility of the broader market. If a stock's volatility, as measured by beta, is high, the stock is considered risky. If volatility is low, the stock is considered less risky.

A beta of 1 corresponds to the risk of the broader market. A beta of 2 means the company is twice as volatile as the overall market, while a beta below 1 means the company is less volatile and carries less risk than the broader market.

## Example of Unlevered Beta

For example, calculating the unlevered beta of Tesla, Inc. (as of November 2017):

- Beta (B_L) = 0.73
- Debt-to-equity (D/E) ratio = 2.2
- Corporate tax rate = 35%

Unlevered beta is nearly always equal to or lower than levered beta, given that debt is typically zero or positive. (In the rare case where a company's debt component is negative -- for example, if the company is hoarding cash -- unlevered beta could exceed levered beta.)

If unlevered beta is positive, investors will invest in the company's stock when prices are expected to rise. A negative unlevered beta will prompt investors to invest when prices are expected to fall.

## How Does Unlevered Beta Help Investors?

Unlevered beta removes any favorable or unfavorable effects that arise from adding debt to a company's capital structure. Comparing unlevered betas across companies allows investors to gain a clearer understanding of the risk they are assuming when purchasing the stock. Because companies differ in capital structure and debt levels, investors can calculate unlevered beta to compare them effectively with one another or against the market. This approach isolates the sensitivity of a company's assets (equity) to the market.

## What Is Beta?

In short, beta is a measure of market risk. More precisely, it measures the volatility -- or systematic risk -- of a security or portfolio relative to the market as a whole. In statistical terms, it is the slope coefficient of a security (stock) regressed against a benchmark market index (the S&P 500). Each data point represents an individual stock's return compared with the overall market's return. Beta thus effectively describes the behavior of a security's returns in response to market fluctuations.

## What Is Levered Beta?

Levered beta measures the risk of a firm with both debt and equity in its capital structure relative to market volatility. A key determinant of beta is leverage, which measures the ratio of a company's debt to its equity. The levered beta of a publicly traded security therefore measures the sensitivity of that security's returns to overall market performance. A levered beta greater than +1 or less than -1 indicates that the security is more volatile than the market. A levered beta between -1 and +1 indicates lower volatility than the market.
