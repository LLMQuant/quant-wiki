# What Is an Underlying Asset?

![](https://fastly.jsdelivr.net/gh/bucketio/img9@main/2024/10/20/1729465031968-b3c8959e-1d37-4b8a-91b1-b0b0dfe25143.png)
An underlying asset is the financial asset upon which a derivative's price is based. Options are one example of a derivative. A derivative is a financial instrument whose price is derived from a different asset.

## Basics of Underlying Assets

Underlying assets give derivatives their value. For example, an option on stock XYZ gives the holder the right to buy or sell XYZ at the strike price until expiration. The underlying asset for that option is the stock of XYZ. Many -- though not all -- stocks have options chains.

An underlying asset can be used to identify the item within an agreement that provides value to the contract. The underlying asset supports the security involved in the agreement, which the parties agree to exchange as part of the derivative contract.

## Understanding Derivative Contracts

The price of an option or futures contract is derived from the price of an underlying asset. In an options contract, the writer must either buy or sell the underlying asset to the buyer on the specified date at the agreed-upon price. The buyer is not obligated to purchase the underlying asset but may exercise that right if they choose to do so. If the option is approaching expiration and the underlying asset has not moved favorably enough to make exercising worthwhile, the buyer can let the option expire and forfeit the premium paid.

Futures contracts create an obligation for both buyer and seller. The seller of the future agrees to deliver the underlying asset at expiration, and the buyer agrees to purchase it. The price received and paid is the price at which the parties entered the futures contract. Most futures traders close their positions before expiration, since retail traders and hedge funds rarely need to take physical possession of, for example, barrels of oil. However, they can buy or sell the contract at one price and, if the price moves favorably, exit the trade at a profit. Futures are derivatives because, for example, the price of an oil futures contract is based on the price movement of oil.

### Key Takeaways

- An underlying asset represents the asset from which a derivative derives its value.
- Knowing the value of the underlying asset helps traders determine the appropriate action on the derivative (buy, sell, or hold).

## Examples of Underlying Assets

In the case of stock options, the underlying asset is the stock itself. For example, a stock option to purchase 100 shares of Company X at a price of $100 has Company X's stock as the underlying asset. The underlying asset is used to determine the value of the option up to expiration. The value of the underlying asset may change before the contract expires, affecting the value of the option. The price of the underlying asset at any given time tells traders whether the option is worth exercising.

The underlying asset can also be a currency or a market index, such as the S&P 500. In the case of stock indices, the underlying asset consists of the common stocks within the market index.
