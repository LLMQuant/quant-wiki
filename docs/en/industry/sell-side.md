## Goldman Sachs

The Goldman Sachs Group, Inc. is an American multinational investment bank and financial services corporation, founded in 1869 and headquartered at 200 West Street in Lower Manhattan, New York City. The firm maintains regional headquarters in London, Warsaw, Bangalore, Hong Kong, Tokyo, Dallas, and Salt Lake City, among other locations. By revenue, Goldman Sachs is the second-largest investment bank in the world, ranks 55th on the Fortune 500, and is designated a systemically important financial institution by the Financial Stability Board (FSB).

**Business and Highlights**

- **Startup Financing**: Goldman Sachs invests in and arranges financing for startups; when these companies later pursue IPOs, Goldman often secures a leading role in the offering.
- **Notable IPOs**: In 2013, Goldman served as a lead underwriter for Twitter's IPO, and participated in numerous high-profile offerings in 2021.
- **Investment Portfolio**: The firm's funds have invested in startups including Spotify, Foodpanda, and Dropbox.
- **World Economic Forum**: Goldman Sachs is a strategic partner of the World Economic Forum (WEF).

## Morgan Stanley

Morgan Stanley is an American multinational investment bank and financial services firm headquartered at 1585 Broadway in Midtown Manhattan, New York City. The company operates in 41 countries with approximately 75,000 employees, serving corporations, governments, institutions, and individuals. Morgan Stanley ranked 61st on the 2021 Fortune 500.

**Business Structure**

- **Three Core Divisions**: Institutional Securities, Wealth Management, and Investment Management.
- **Capabilities**: Through its subsidiaries and affiliates, Morgan Stanley provides comprehensive financial services spanning underwriting, trading, capital allocation, and financial planning for institutional, governmental, and individual clients.

## JPMorgan Chase

JPMorgan Chase & Co. is an American multinational financial services corporation incorporated in Delaware and headquartered in New York City. It is the largest bank in the United States and the world's largest bank by market capitalization (as of 2023), designated as a systemically important financial institution by the FSB. Its massive scale entails heightened regulatory scrutiny and the maintenance of what the firm calls its "fortress balance sheet." The company currently operates from 383 Madison Avenue in Midtown Manhattan and plans to relocate to a new building by 2025.

**Core Strengths**

- **Asset Scale**: Total assets of $3.67 trillion, making it the world's fifth-largest bank.
- **Investment Banking**: The world's largest investment bank by revenue.
- **Rankings**: Ranks 24th on the Fortune 500.
- **Research and Influence**: Provides institutional research across financial markets, economics, geopolitics, and personal finance, with strong brand loyalty and significant market share. The firm has also faced scrutiny regarding risk management, financing activities, and major legal settlements.

## BofA Securities

BofA Securities, Inc. is the investment banking division of Bank of America, formerly known as Bank of America Merrill Lynch. It is distinct from Merrill -- Bank of America's brokerage and trading subsidiary.

**Merger History**

- **January 2009**: Bank of America acquired Merrill Lynch, merging their corporate and investment banking operations to form today's BofA Securities.
- **Service Scope**: Includes M&A, equity and debt capital markets, lending, trading, risk management, research, liquidity, and payment services.

---

## Citigroup

Citigroup Inc. (commonly Citi) is an American multinational investment bank and financial services corporation headquartered in New York. The company was formed in 1998 through the merger of Citicorp (Citibank) and financial conglomerate Travelers Group, the latter of which was divested in 2002. Citi is incorporated in Delaware.

**Industry Position**

- **Third-Largest U.S. Bank**: Alongside JPMorgan Chase, Bank of America, and Wells Fargo, Citi is one of the "Big Four" American banks.
- **Systemic Importance**: Designated as a systemically important bank by the FSB and widely considered "too big to fail."
- **Investment Banking**: Citi is a member of the global "Bulge Bracket" investment banks.

## Barclays

Barclays is a British multinational universal bank headquartered in London, structured around two core divisions -- Barclays UK and Barclays International -- with Barclays Execution Services providing support.

- **Barclays UK**: Encompasses retail banking, credit cards, wealth management, and UK corporate banking.
- **Barclays International**: Comprises Barclays Corporate and Investment Bank (formerly Barclays Capital) alongside consumer, cards, and payments businesses. The investment bank serves large corporate, institutional, and governmental clients with advisory, financing, and risk management services, and acts as a primary dealer in UK Gilts, U.S. Treasuries, and multiple European sovereign bond markets.

## UBS

UBS Group AG is a multinational investment bank and financial services company headquartered in Zurich and Basel, Switzerland. It is Switzerland's largest bank and one of the world's leading private banks. With substantial operations across the Americas, Europe, the Middle East, and Asia-Pacific, UBS is designated as a systemically important bank.

- **Business Scope**: Beyond private banking, UBS offers wealth management, asset management, and investment banking services to individual, corporate, and institutional clients.
- **Market Position**: Manages the world's largest volume of private wealth, with a substantial base of ultra-high-net-worth clients. Despite scaling back some sell-side operations, UBS maintains its global investment banking and major market-making presence.
- **Controversies**: Due to Switzerland's stringent banking secrecy laws, UBS has been subject to tax evasion investigations in the United States, France, Germany, Israel, and Belgium.

## Deutsche Bank

Deutsche Bank AG is a German multinational investment bank and financial services company headquartered in Frankfurt, dual-listed on the Frankfurt Stock Exchange and the New York Stock Exchange.

- **History and Acquisitions**: Founded in 1870; acquired Disconto-Gesellschaft in 1929, Bankers Trust in 1998, and Deutsche Postbank in 2010.
- **Scale**: As of 2018, the bank operated across 58 countries with significant presence in Europe, the Americas, and Asia.
- **Market Standing**: Widely regarded as Germany's largest bank, Deutsche Bank has been designated a global systemically important bank by the FSB since 2011.

---

## BNP Paribas

BNP Paribas is a French multinational universal banking and financial services holding company, formed in 2000 through the merger of Banque Nationale de Paris (BNP) and Paribas. With approximately 190,000 employees, the firm operates across three main divisions: Commercial, Personal Banking & Services (CPBS), Investment & Protection Services (IPS), and Corporate & Institutional Banking (CIB).

- **Market Position**: The largest bank in Europe and the ninth-largest globally by assets. Following the 2008 financial crisis, BNP Paribas rose to rank among the world's top five banks.
- **Systemic Importance**: Designated as a systemically important bank by the FSB; ranked 33rd globally on the Forbes Global 2000 list.

---

## HSBC

HSBC Holdings plc is a British multinational universal banking and financial services group headquartered in London, with deep historical and commercial ties to East Asia and a global operating footprint. By total assets, HSBC was Europe's largest bank as of the end of 2021, surpassing BNP Paribas.

- **Network**: Operations span 64 countries and territories across six continents, serving approximately 40 million customers.
- **Rankings**: Ranked 20th on the 2023 Forbes Global list of major corporations.
- **Listings**: Dual primary-listed on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange and the London Stock Exchange; constituent of both the Hang Seng Index and the FTSE 100 Index, with additional listings on the NYSE and the Bermuda Stock Exchange.

---

## Wells Fargo

Wells Fargo & Company is an American multinational financial services corporation with global operations spanning 35 countries and serving over 70 million customers. The FSB designates Wells Fargo as a systemically important financial institution, and it is one of the "Big Four" U.S. banks alongside JPMorgan Chase, Bank of America, and Citigroup.

- **Investment Products**: Offered through subsidiaries Wells Fargo Investments, LLC and Wells Fargo Advisors, LLC, as well as nationwide brokerage operations.
- **Investment Banking Division**: Wells Fargo Securities (WFS) is headquartered in Charlotte, with trading operations in Manhattan and regional offices in Minneapolis, Boston, Houston, San Francisco, and Los Angeles. International offices are located in London, Hong Kong, Singapore, and Tokyo. Established in 2009 following the acquisition of Wachovia Securities, WFS provides sales and trading, fixed income, FX and rates, equities, M&A advisory, high yield, leveraged finance, equity underwriting, private placements, loan syndication, risk management, and public finance services.

---

## Jefferies Group

Jefferies Group LLC is an American multinational, independent investment bank and financial services firm headquartered in New York City. The firm provides capital markets and financial advisory services, institutional brokerage, securities research, and asset management, covering M&A, restructuring, and other advisory areas. Its Capital Markets business also encompasses securities trading and investment banking activities through its indirect majority-owned subsidiary, Jefferies High Yield Trading, LLC.
