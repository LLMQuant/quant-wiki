# Quadrature Capital: The Secretive Proprietary Trading Firm You Have Never Heard Of

![](https://fastly.jsdelivr.net/gh/bucketio/img9@main/2024/10/20/1729465031968-b3c8959e-1d37-4b8a-91b1-b0b0dfe25143.png)

Who is Quadrature Capital? What exactly is it? Is it a hedge fund? A high-frequency proprietary trading firm? Or a special vehicle that generates enormous profits in order to fight climate change?

In an era of increasing transparency in the financial world, **Quadrature** remains unusually opaque.

## They Do Not Respond -- At Least Not Much

Quadrature Capital is deeply reticent about external inquiries. Multiple attempts at contact have gone unanswered. Their website tersely describes the firm as a "technology-driven business" that uses statistical models to conduct market-neutral trading with its own capital. Yet the company's most recent regulatory filings describe it as providing "investment management services" and charging fees on assets under management.

Rumors abound: some say the firm hoards GPUs like XTX to power AI-driven trading algorithms; others describe lavish company retreats, office beer on tap, and an internal culture compared to "Oompa Loompas" -- referring to the fictional workers in *Charlie and the Chocolate Factory* who, once they join, never leave.

One thing is beyond dispute: they pay exceptionally well and donate generously to the Quadrature Climate Foundation.

## The Indisputable Facts: High Pay and Climate Philanthropy

Official filings published by Companies House show that Quadrature Capital Limited paid its UK-based employees an average of 2.46 million pounds last year. While down from the 2022 peak of 3.6 million pounds, this figure remains extraordinary.

The firm's two billionaire co-founders, Greg Skinner and Suneil Setiya, do not draw salaries from the company. Instead, they took combined loans of 56.7 million pounds last year through a tax-efficient arrangement. Meanwhile, the Quadrature Climate Foundation received 269 million pounds in donations in 2023 and paid an average of 293,000 pounds to its 14 employees. Setiya frequently posts on social media urging collective action against climate change, arguing that humanity is on a trajectory of "excessive emissions" that must be curtailed immediately.

## The Mysterious Work Environment

![](https://fastly.jsdelivr.net/gh/bucketio/img19@main/2024/12/17/1734452479267-b5a84f08-1ad6-4f94-b2e9-3d25123929d5.png)

While Quadrature's high pay and environmental commitments are documented in public filings, its work environment is shrouded in mystery. The company's website highlights its "lush green" office setting. The London office features a large gym and meeting rooms themed as "ski lodge," "lakeside," and "beach." A Singapore office opened in 2023, and a New York office launched in September 2024. Employees enjoy music lessons and free books, along with annual summer and winter trips -- the carbon footprint of which is reportedly offset.

Yet accounts of what actually goes on inside are varied and unverified:

- Some say annual trips involve private jets, requiring additional carbon offsets; others claim these are simply picnics in a nearby park.
- Some assert the office has beer on tap like WeWork, while an insider insists nobody actually drinks it.
- Persistent rumors describe a culture of extreme secrecy, with employees rigorously adhering to NDAs.

One person with knowledge of the firm said: "Quadrature is a bit of a 'cult,' and that's very much by design. The company treats people exceptionally well, with fantastic perks. Many people would stay even for less money."

## A Small, Elite Team

Quadrature is not a giant. By comparison, Jane Street employs over 2,600 people and generated $14 billion in net trading revenue in nine months. Quadrature Capital Limited produced 588 million pounds in revenue in the 12 months ending January 2024, with 143 employees -- relatively modest in scale.

![](https://fastly.jsdelivr.net/gh/bucketio/img6@main/2024/12/17/1734452510827-a9ce47dc-15a9-444c-a863-3bebe97e4856.png)

If you want to join Quadrature, sources say talent recruitment is managed by Ada Offonry, who joined in 2021 as head of talent acquisition and is an advocate for diversity in hiring. The firm is also active in hiring interns, managed by Hannah Ewert, who joined from Meta in 2021.

## Talent Retention: Few Leave

Most people who join Quadrature appear to stay. But there are exceptions: for instance, unconfirmed rumors point to friction between the technology and operations departments. Laurent Mazare, a former Jane Street and DeepMind researcher who joined in June 2023, departed just four months later in September 2023. Mike White, a former WorldQuant CTO who joined in May 2023, left in October. Were they let go? Was it a poor fit? No one knows, and Quadrature has no interest in explaining.

## A Quiet Rise

A senior quantitative professional recalls: "I met them around 2011 -- it was just four people in a basement somewhere in the City of London. A few years later, they had moved into a high-rise with a large staff and had quietly grown into something substantial."

Quadrature has thrived in its garden-surrounded offices while maintaining an extremely low profile. If you are a quantitative researcher, the word is that it is an attractive place -- high pay, generous employee benefits, a clear environmental mission, very low turnover, and meaningful scale, all achieved with minimal public attention.

Perhaps that is precisely the strategy: **quietly scaling up within a "secret garden," without needing to say a word to the outside world.**
