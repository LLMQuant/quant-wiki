## High-Frequency Trading

### Hudson River Trading (HRT)

Hudson River Trading (HRT) is a quantitative trading firm headquartered in New York City, founded in 2002. By 2014, the firm accounted for approximately 5% of all U.S. trading volume. HRT employs over 800 people across offices in New York, Chicago, Austin, Boulder, London, Singapore, Shanghai, Mumbai, and Dublin. The firm focuses on developing automated trading algorithms using mathematical techniques and trades across more than 100 global markets.

HRT operates across multiple asset classes with varying holding periods. Unlike typical high-frequency trading (HFT) firms, HRT holds approximately 25% of its trading capital overnight (most HFT firms hold virtually no overnight positions), maintains an average holding period of about five minutes (compared to milliseconds for some HFT firms), and conducts less than 1% of its trading volume in dark pools.

---

### Tower Research Capital

Tower Research Capital LLC (commonly known as Tower Research) is a fund company engaged in high-frequency trading, algorithmic trading, and financial services. One of the earliest automated trading firms, Tower Research was founded in February 1998 by Mark Gorton and Alistair Brown.

Tower's individual trading teams operate independently with autonomy while sharing technology resources such as hardware, software, and network connectivity, as well as business management, legal compliance, and risk management support. The engineering team is central to operations, responsible for the architecture, implementation, and optimization of the entire trading platform -- including systems for market data access, trend analysis, trade simulation, order entry and management, live trading support, risk management, and post-trade services.

Through this collaborative structure, Tower deploys diversified automated and quantitative strategies across multiple asset classes on global markets around the clock. As of 2022, Tower employs over 1,000 people worldwide with diverse backgrounds spanning mathematics, computer science, physics, law, economics, engineering, and finance.

### Jump Trading

Jump Trading LLC is a proprietary trading firm specializing in algorithmic and high-frequency trading strategies. The company employs over 700 people across offices in Chicago, New York, Austin, London, Tel Aviv, Singapore, Shanghai, Bristol, Gurgaon, Gandhinagar, Sydney, Amsterdam, Hong Kong, and Paris, actively trading in futures, options, cryptocurrencies, and equity markets globally. Jump has operated exclusively with proprietary capital since its founding.

The firm is a member of the Principal Traders Group, an advisory organization established by the Futures Industry Association (FIA) to represent firms that trade solely with their own capital.

### Virtu Financial

Virtu Financial is an American company providing financial services, trading products, and market-making services. Virtu's product suite includes execution, liquidity sourcing, analytics, and broker-neutral, multi-dealer workflow technology. The firm provides two-sided quotations and trading in equities, commodities, currencies, options, fixed income, and other securities across more than 230 exchanges, markets, and dark pools. Virtu uses proprietary technology to trade large volumes of securities and went public on NASDAQ in 2015.

Virtu operates across 36 countries, over 235 exchanges, markets, and dark pools, including NYSE Euronext, NASDAQ, and the Chicago Mercantile Exchange. The company is a designated market maker on the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) and NYSE Amex, making markets in over 12,000 securities and other financial instruments. Offices are located in New York City (headquarters), Austin, Los Angeles, Boston, Chicago, London, Sydney, Dublin, and Singapore.

### Quantlab

Quantlab is an American quantitative proprietary trading firm headquartered in Houston, Texas, with additional offices in New York, Chicago, Boston, Austin, Denver, and Singapore.

Founded in 1998 by Wilbur "Ed" Bosarge, Jr. (a former mathematics professor at Rice University) and Bruce Eames (a businessman from Wisconsin who had worked at Boston Consulting Group), the firm initially hired several mathematics Ph.D.s. The team developed algorithms for high-frequency trading and customized code for new asset classes and markets. During its first decade through 2015, Quantlab was a major player in HFT, accounting for approximately 3% of NYSE trading volume on some days. During this period, the firm accumulated over $3 billion in profits, with more than 70% going to Bosarge.

### Tradebot Systems, Inc.

Tradebot Systems, Inc. is an American high-frequency equity trading company headquartered in Kansas City, Missouri, typically accounting for about 5% of total U.S. stock market volume. According to founder Dave Cummings, as of 2008 the firm "usually only held a stock for 11 seconds" and "had not had a losing day in 4 years." This streak of consecutive profitable days continued until it was broken in 2017.

### HAP Capital

HAP Capital's market-making activities rely on proprietary applications that make pricing decisions in a consistent, rational, and disciplined manner, competing for trading opportunities through superior quotes and execution speed. As a provider of liquidity and efficiency to markets, the firm identifies demand for specific equity and index options.

Through its portfolio managers, HAP Capital accelerates price discovery while extracting expected returns from an environment designed to facilitate and reward verifiable alpha and well-considered risk-taking. With over a decade of consistent success, HAP Capital distinguishes itself from most peers through its internally developed quantitative approach to trading strategies and business models.

## Proprietary Trading / Market Making

### Citadel Securities

Citadel Securities is an American market-making firm headquartered in Miami and one of the largest market makers globally, operating in over 50 countries. It is the largest designated market maker on the New York Stock Exchange. Citadel Securities and hedge fund Citadel LLC operate independently, though both were founded and are majority-owned by Kenneth C. Griffin.

Citadel Securities' automated systems improve trading reliability, reduce costs, and narrow spreads. In 2015, *Barron's* named it the market maker providing the best price improvement for investors in both S&P 500 and non-S&P stocks. The firm is also the largest U.S. options market maker, executing approximately 25% of all U.S. listed equity options volume. According to *The Wall Street Journal*, roughly one-third of retail stock orders are executed through Citadel Securities (accounting for about 10% of its revenue), and the firm executes approximately 13% of consolidated U.S. equity volume and 28% of U.S. retail equity volume.

---

### Jane Street Capital

Jane Street Capital (commonly known as Jane Street) is a global proprietary trading firm with over 2,000 employees across five offices in New York, London, Hong Kong, Amsterdam, and Singapore. The firm trades multiple asset classes across more than 200 venues in 45 countries and traded over $17 trillion in securities in 2020. During the 2020 market turmoil, Jane Street was recognized for playing an important role in maintaining bond ETF liquidity.

Jane Street is a global liquidity provider and trading firm that maintains consistent and reliable pricing through deep quantitative analysis and thorough understanding of market mechanics. The firm trades exclusively with proprietary capital, providing strategic flexibility and sustainability across market cycles. Its Capital Markets Group invests firm capital in longer-term, fundamentals-driven opportunities across private and public companies, while the Fundamental Research team provides detailed feedback and valuation perspectives. Jane Street leverages multidisciplinary expertise to price large, complex trades.

### Optiver

Optiver is a proprietary trading firm and market maker dealing in various financial instruments listed on exchanges worldwide. The name derives from the Dutch word "optieverhandelaar" (meaning "options trader"). The privately held company trades options, futures, equities, FX, fixed income, and commodities, with offices in Amsterdam, New York, Chicago, Taipei, Shanghai, London, Singapore, and other locations, employing over 1,600 people.

### Five Rings

Five Rings is a proprietary trading firm founded with the vision of combining strategy, innovation, and technology to succeed in today's global markets. Headquartered in New York, the firm operates in both established and niche domestic and international markets without relying on clients or outside investors. The team continuously seeks new opportunities, evaluates risk-reward profiles, and develops corresponding strategies and tools. Its competitive advantage stems primarily from exceptional talent, which is reflected in its compensation structure.

Five Rings maintains a relaxed atmosphere with flat hierarchies and strong benefits, emphasizing the importance of education. Senior traders and software developers provide extensive hands-on and classroom training for new employees. As a young, rapidly growing firm, there is ample room for advancement for those who embrace the challenge.

### IMC Financial Markets

IMC Financial Markets (sometimes referred to as IMC Trading) is a proprietary trading firm and market maker providing liquidity for a variety of financial instruments listed on global exchanges. Founded in 1989 as International Marketmakers Combination, the firm now employs over 1,100 people, headquartered in Amsterdam with offices in Chicago, Sydney, Hong Kong, Mumbai, and other locations.

IMC is a technology-driven trading firm active on more than 100 trading venues worldwide, providing liquidity for over 200,000 securities. The firm's primary trading products include equities, bonds, commodities, and currencies, and it plays an important liquidity provider role on prominent markets such as NYSE Arca, NASDAQ, CBOE, BATS, and CME.

### DRW Trading Group

DRW Holdings, LLC (commonly known as DRW) is a diversified trading firm that continuously innovates across traditional and emerging markets. Headquartered in Chicago, the company was founded in 1992 by Don Wilson, a former options trader at the Chicago Mercantile Exchange, and is named after his initials. DRW trades financial instruments including fixed income, options and derivatives, energy and agricultural commodities, and cryptocurrencies, with offices in Amsterdam, Austin, Greenwich, Tel Aviv, Chicago, New York, Houston, London, Montreal, and Singapore. It is among the top five largest global trading firms.

DRW employs a variety of strategies including high-frequency trading. It was mentioned in Michael Lewis's 2014 book *Flash Boys*, which described trading firms competing for nanosecond-level latency advantages. DRW also actively participates in cryptocurrency and blockchain-related ventures.

QRT combines data, research, technology, and trading expertise into a collaborative mindset aimed at solving the most complex problems. ### GTS

GTS is a market maker covering equities, ETFs, commodities, futures, foreign exchange, and interest rate products. The firm trades over 10,000 products globally, executing millions of trades daily, leveraging years of responsible, technology-driven trading experience to improve financial market efficiency. Positioned at the intersection of capital markets and cutting-edge technology, GTS's innovations deliver better price discovery, trade execution, and transparency for investors.

GTS accounts for approximately 3-5% of daily U.S. equity cash market volume and is the largest designated market maker on the New York Stock Exchange, managing over $11 trillion in market capitalization.

### Flow Traders

Flow Traders is a proprietary trading firm and market maker that uses high-frequency and quantitative trading strategies to provide liquidity to securities markets. Originally established in Amsterdam, the firm has offices in New York, London, Milan, Paris, Cluj, Shanghai, Singapore, and Hong Kong. Flow Traders continuously provides two-sided quotes for ETPs (exchange-traded products), options, fixed income products, and currencies. As one of the world's largest ETP market makers, Flow Traders provides liquidity across more than 13,000 ETP products in North America, Europe, and Asia-Pacific. As of 2020, Flow Traders was the largest ETF market maker in Europe.

## Hedge Funds

### Citadel LLC

Citadel LLC (formerly Citadel Investment Group, LLC) is an American multinational hedge fund and financial services company. Founded by Ken Griffin in 1990, it managed over $62 billion in assets as of December 2022. The firm employs more than 2,600 people, with headquarters in Miami, Florida, and offices across North America, Asia, and Europe. Founder, CEO, and Co-Chief Investment Officer Griffin holds approximately 85% of the company. As of December 2022, Citadel was considered the most successful hedge fund in history, having earned $65.9 billion in net gains since its 1990 inception. Citadel LLC and Citadel Securities operate independently. Citadel invests across five main strategies: equities, commodities, fixed income, quantitative strategies, and credit.

---

### Renaissance Technologies

Renaissance Technologies LLC (also known as RenTech or RenTec) is an American hedge fund headquartered in East Setauket, Long Island, New York, specializing in systematic trading using quantitative models based on mathematical and statistical analysis. Its flagship Medallion fund is renowned for the best investment track record in history.

Founded in 1982 by mathematician James Simons, a former Cold War codebreaker, the Medallion fund -- available primarily to firm employees -- achieved annualized returns exceeding 66% before fees (approximately 39% after fees) over 30 years (1988-2018), earning it the title of "the best record on Wall Street." The firm also offers two funds to external investors: Renaissance Institutional Equities Fund (RIEF) and Renaissance Institutional Diversified Alpha (RIDA). Given Renaissance's remarkable success, particularly the Medallion fund, Simons has been described as "the greatest money manager on earth."

---

### D. E. Shaw & Co.

D. E. Shaw & Co., L.P. is a multinational investment management firm founded in 1988 by David E. Shaw in New York City. The firm is known for developing sophisticated mathematical models and advanced computer programs to exploit financial market anomalies. As of June 2021, D. E. Shaw managed $55 billion, with $35 billion in alternative investments and $20 billion in long-biased investments. In 2018, *Institutional Investor* reported that D. E. Shaw ranked fifth globally among hedge funds by lifetime returns.

The firm manages multiple investment funds, relying on extensive quantitative methods and proprietary computational technology to support fundamental research. It also uses qualitative analysis for private equity investments in technology, wind energy, real estate, financial services, and distressed companies.

---

### Point72 Asset Management

Point72 Asset Management is an American hedge fund founded in 2014 by Steve Cohen, having evolved from S.A.C. Capital Advisors (which had pleaded guilty to insider trading charges). The firm reopened to external investors in 2018 after a two-year ban. Headquartered in Stamford, Connecticut, it maintains offices in New York, Hong Kong, Tokyo, Singapore, London, Paris, Sydney, Warsaw, and Palo Alto.

The firm operates across multiple strategies including long/short equity (Point72, Ever Point), systematic investing (Cubist Systematic Strategies), global macro, and venture capital (Point72 Ventures).

---

### Two Sigma

Two Sigma Investments is a hedge fund headquartered in New York City that employs artificial intelligence, machine learning, distributed computing, and other technological approaches to develop trading strategies. Managed by John Overdeck and David Siegel, Two Sigma is frequently mentioned alongside D. E. Shaw and Renaissance Technologies for its comparable investment returns. In October 2014, Two Sigma raised $3.3 billion for its macro hedge fund, one of the largest such raises since the 2008 financial crisis.

The firm operates specialized divisions focused on private or venture capital investments (primarily in data science), provides advisory services to institutional clients, and runs a high-frequency proprietary trading operation, with offices across North America, Europe, and Asia.

---

### Millennium Management, LLC

Millennium Management is a multi-strategy hedge fund investment management firm. As of June 2023, it manages over $58.9 billion in assets across the Americas, Europe, and Asia. The firm operates a platform model with approximately 280 independent investment teams, each allocated capital to deploy diversified trading strategies. With offices in 17 global locations including London, Singapore, Miami, Bangalore, Tel Aviv, Tokyo, Dublin, Greenwich, and Paris, its primary base is New York. Core strategies encompass RV fundamental equity, equity arbitrage, fixed income strategies, and quantitative strategies.

---

### Bridgewater Associates

Bridgewater Associates, LP (commonly "Bridgewater") is an American investment management firm founded by Ray Dalio in 1975. It serves institutional clients including pension funds, endowments, foundations, foreign governments, and central banks. Known for its global macro investment style, the firm makes investments based on macroeconomic trends such as inflation, currency movements, and U.S. GDP.

Starting as an institutional advisory service in 1975, Bridgewater pioneered the risk parity investment approach in 1996. The firm relocated its headquarters from New York City to Westport, Connecticut in 1981 and currently employs 1,500 people. Bridgewater positions itself as a "global macro firm" using "quantitative" investment methods, aiming to build portfolios with uncorrelated returns by allocating based on risk weighting rather than asset classes. The firm typically only accepts large institutional clients.

---

### Balyasny Asset Management

Balyasny Asset Management is an American investment management firm headquartered in Chicago, with additional offices in Canada, London, and Asia. Founded in 2001 by Dmitry Balyasny, Scott Schroeder, and Taylor O'Malley, the firm initially focused on long/short equity strategies, which accounted for 70% of the firm's risk. Over its first 16 years, Balyasny had few losing periods and achieved annualized returns of approximately 12%.

### Capula Investment Management

Capula Investment Management LLP is a British hedge fund and the fourth-largest in Europe, managing approximately $23 billion in assets as of H2 2020. Headquartered at 7 Clarges Street in London's Mayfair district, Capula has offices in Hong Kong, Japan, Singapore, and the United States. The firm manages absolute return, enhanced fixed income, macro, and crisis alpha strategies.

---

### AQR Capital

AQR Capital Management (Applied Quantitative Research) is a global investment management firm headquartered in Greenwich, Connecticut. Founded in 1998 by Cliff Asness, David Kabiller, John Liew, and Robert Krail, AQR offers a range of quantitatively driven alternative and traditional investment products to institutional clients and financial advisors.

With additional offices in Boston, Chicago, Los Angeles, Bangalore, Hong Kong, London, Sydney, and Tokyo, AQR advocates a research-based, "systematic and consistent" approach to portfolio construction while emphasizing broad diversification with strategies that have low correlation to traditional assets. AQR was an early adopter of factor (style) investing, combining value, momentum, defensive, and carry styles to exploit diversification benefits. The firm is also known for alternative investment strategies such as risk parity, managed futures, and trend following.

---

### Man Group

Man Group plc is an active investment management company listed on the London Stock Exchange, providing a range of funds to global institutional and private investors across liquid and illiquid markets. It is the world's largest publicly traded hedge fund company, managing approximately $151.4 billion as of March 2022. Headquartered at Riverbank House in London with over 1,400 employees globally, the company was sponsor of the Man Booker literary prize from 2002 to 2019.

Man Group operates five investment engines covering quantitative and discretionary strategies, trading in more than 800 global markets, offering over 75 investment strategies, and employing more than 500 quantitative and technology professionals.

---

### Davidson Kempner Capital Management

Davidson Kempner Capital Management LP is a global institutional alternative investment management firm managing over $36 billion in assets. Headquartered in New York City with offices in London, Hong Kong, Dublin, Philadelphia, Shenzhen, and Mumbai, the firm employs approximately 500 people. Led by Executive Managing Partner and CIO Anthony A. Yoseloff, Davidson Kempner primarily employs bottom-up fundamental analysis with a focus on event-driven strategies, investing across equity and debt through a multi-strategy approach in global markets.

### Farallon Capital

Farallon Capital Management, L.L.C. is an American multi-strategy hedge fund headquartered in San Francisco, California. Founded by Tom Steyer in 1986, the firm employs approximately 230 professionals across eight countries. Farallon manages approximately $39 billion in assets, sourced primarily from university endowments, foundations, and high-net-worth individuals. The firm claims to have pioneered the absolute return investment model, focusing on both potential returns and risk-adjusted returns. Investment areas include value investing, credit, merger arbitrage, real estate, and direct investments in developed and emerging markets.

### Elliott Investment Management

Elliott Investment Management is an American investment management firm and one of the world's largest activist funds. It manages Elliott Associates L.P. and Elliott International Limited. Founded by Paul Singer in New York, the firm's portfolio exceeded $8 billion as of Q1 2015. The company is currently closed to new investors and is one of the longest continuously operating hedge funds.

### Squarepoint Capital

Squarepoint Capital is a global investment management firm headquartered in New York City with additional offices in Europe and Asia. The firm uses quantitative finance methods to invest across diversified holdings, multiple asset classes, trading frequencies, and global markets. Most investment decisions are derived from backtesting strategies against historical market data.

---

### PDT Partners

PDT Partners (Process Driven Trading Partners) is a hedge fund led by Peter Muller. Originally established in 1993 within Morgan Stanley's trading division, it became an independent firm in 2012. With approximately 250 employees in New York and London, PDT is organized into research, technology, and business operations teams. The research team builds complex mathematical models to predict market movements; the technology team implements these models in automated trading systems; and the business operations team ensures the firm operates in a robust, efficient, and compliant environment.

### TGS Management

TGS Management (TGS) is an American quantitative investment management firm founded in 1989, with offices in Princeton, New Jersey and Irvine, California. The firm maintains an extremely low profile but is known for its highly selective recruitment of candidates with strong quantitative backgrounds, such as software engineers and Ph.D. holders.

### WorldQuant

WorldQuant, LLC is an international hedge fund and quantitative investment management firm headquartered in Old Greenwich, Connecticut. Founded in 2007, the firm currently manages approximately $9 billion in assets for Millennium Management through quantitative trading and other quantitative investment approaches. WorldQuant has hosted the WorldQuant Challenge (a competition in quantitative finance) and the WorldQuant Accelerator (an independent portfolio management platform). As of April 2017, WorldQuant had more than 500 full-time employees and 450 paid research consultants, providing portfolio management, market research, and trading services.

## Asset Management

### BlackRock

BlackRock, Inc. is an American multinational investment company headquartered in New York City. Founded in 1988 as a risk management and fixed-income institutional asset manager, BlackRock is the world's largest asset manager, with $8.59 trillion under management as of December 31, 2022. The firm operates 70 offices in 30 countries, serving clients in 100 countries. BlackRock also manages the iShares ETF family and, alongside Vanguard and State Street, is considered one of the "Big Three" index fund providers. Its Aladdin software provides portfolio management for many major financial institutions. BlackRock ranks 184th on the Fortune 500.

BlackRock has sought a leadership role in ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) investing, though it has also faced scrutiny regarding its involvement in fossil fuels, weapons, and human rights issues in China. Several U.S. states have divested from or refused to work with BlackRock due to its ESG policies.

---

### The Vanguard Group

The Vanguard Group, Inc. is an American registered investment advisor based in Malvern, Pennsylvania. As of April 2023, Vanguard managed approximately $7.7 trillion globally, making it the world's largest mutual fund provider and the second-largest ETF provider after BlackRock's iShares. Beyond mutual funds and ETFs, Vanguard offers brokerage services, education accounts, financial planning, asset management, and trust services. Together with BlackRock and State Street, Vanguard is considered one of the "Big Three" index fund providers.

Most Vanguard funds offer both investor shares and admiral shares (with lower fees but higher minimum investments). Headquartered in suburban Philadelphia, the firm has offices across the U.S., Canada, Australia, Asia, and Europe.

---

### Fidelity Investments

Fidelity Investments (also known as Fidelity or FMR) is a Boston-based American multinational financial services corporation founded in 1946. It is one of the world's largest asset management companies, managing $4.3 trillion in assets (as of late 2022) and $10.3 trillion in total assets under administration. The firm operates brokerage, mutual funds, fund distribution, investment advisory, retirement services, index funds, wealth management, and securities custody and clearing businesses.

Fidelity's largest equity mutual fund, the Fidelity Contrafund ($107.4 billion), is the largest non-indexed fund in the United States and one of the largest managed by a single fund manager (William Danoff, since 1990).

---

### State Street Global Advisors

State Street Global Advisors (SSGA) is the investment management division of State Street Corporation, managing approximately $4.14 trillion in assets as of December 31, 2021 -- the world's fourth-largest asset manager. SSGA serves institutional clients including governments, corporations, endowments, non-profit foundations, and financial advisors worldwide, with approximately 2,500 employees across 28 countries.

SSGA offers institutional investment strategies through common trust funds and, in the U.S., manages ETFs tracking domestic and international market cap, style, sector, and commodity indices. The firm also offers mutual funds across money market, bond, equity, and multi-asset allocation categories.

---

### PIMCO

PIMCO (Pacific Investment Management Company LLC) is an American investment management company specializing in active management of global fixed income. It manages assets across bonds, equities, commodities, asset allocation, ETFs, hedge funds, and private equity, serving global central banks, sovereign wealth funds, pension funds, corporations, foundations, endowments, and individuals. Headquartered in Newport Beach, California, PIMCO has over 3,100 employees across 22 offices in the Americas, Europe, and Asia.

---

### Invesco

Invesco Ltd. is an American independent investment management company headquartered in Atlanta, Georgia, with offices in 20 countries. Its common stock is an S&P 500 constituent listed on the New York Stock Exchange. Invesco's brands include Invesco, Trimark, Invesco Perpetual, WL Ross & Co, and PowerShares, with strong capabilities in mutual funds, ETFs, and other investment vehicles.

---

### AllianceBernstein

AllianceBernstein Holding L.P. (AB) is a global asset management company providing investment management and research services to institutional, high-net-worth, and retail investors worldwide. The firm relocated its global headquarters from midtown Manhattan to Nashville, Tennessee, and maintains offices across 46 locations in 22 countries. As of December 31, 2021, AB managed approximately $779 billion in assets.

AB provides investment services to institutions while also offering mutual funds, managed accounts, college savings, retirement savings, and insurance services to financial advisors and individual investors. Sell-side research and brokerage are provided through subsidiary Sanford C. Bernstein (Bernstein Research).

---

### Arrowstreet Capital

Arrowstreet Capital is a Boston-based investment management firm offering global and international equity investment strategies and fund products to institutional investors including pension funds, endowments, foundations, and pooled investment vehicles. The firm manages approximately $100 billion for over 200 clients across North America, the UK, Europe, and Asia-Pacific.

Arrowstreet provides a focused set of global equity strategies -- long-only, alpha extension, and long/short -- using instruments including swaps and futures. Its quantitative investment process centers on identifying investment signals and incorporating them into proprietary return, risk, and transaction cost models.

---

### PanAgora Asset Management

PanAgora Asset Management is a quantitative investment management firm whose proprietary methods seek to exploit various market inefficiencies across cycles, generating both relative and absolute returns across equity, multi-asset, and risk premium strategies. PanAgora's philosophy combines fundamental investment theses with original research within advanced quantitative frameworks. Founded in 1989 and headquartered in Boston, its shareholders include employees and Great-West Life of Canada.

---

### Winton Group

Winton Group, Ltd (including Winton Capital Management) is a British investment management firm founded by David Harding. The firm trades in over 100 futures markets and equity markets worldwide, with headquarters in London and offices in New York, Hong Kong, Shanghai, Sydney, Abu Dhabi, and other locations.
