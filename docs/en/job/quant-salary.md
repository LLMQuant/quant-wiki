# Global Quant Compensation Guide: Where Do You Stand?

The quantitative finance industry has experienced remarkable growth in recent years. From high-frequency trading to machine learning-driven prediction models, technological advances have fueled demand for specialized quant talent. However, compensation structures vary significantly across regions. From the premium salaries in New York to emerging opportunities in China, here is a comprehensive breakdown of quant compensation worldwide.

![](https://fastly.jsdelivr.net/gh/bucketio/img13@main/2024/12/10/1733789089624-e303bf36-d475-4e34-b0b4-edc45db53b1a.png)

---

## 1. United States: The Global Leader in Quant Compensation

The United States is the birthplace of the quant industry and remains the primary destination for quantitative talent worldwide. Top hedge funds and trading firms compete aggressively for the best candidates through generous compensation and benefits.

- **Junior Quant Analyst (PhD graduate)**: Base salary $150k-$200k, total compensation up to $750k.
- **Senior Researcher or Trader**: At top hedge funds, base salary can exceed $325k, with total compensation reaching $1.4M.
- **Portfolio Manager**: Base salary $200k-$300k, plus a 10%-20% PnL share.

**Key Trends**:

- **Two-year compensation guarantees**: To mitigate turnover risk, many firms offer two-year guaranteed packages, providing candidates with greater stability.
- **Technical and quantitative skill convergence**: Candidates proficient in Python, C++, and ML are in exceptionally high demand.
- **Regional pay disparities**: Salaries in New York are significantly higher than in the Midwest, though cost of living is correspondingly elevated.

---

## 2. Europe: Notable Increases in Total Compensation

The European market features strict non-compete agreements, prompting firms to attract talent through substantial signing bonuses and other incentive mechanisms.

- **Junior Analyst (PhD graduate)**: Base salary GBP 85k-150k, total compensation up to GBP 250k.
- **Managing Director level**: Base salary GBP 250k-400k, total compensation up to GBP 1.2M.
- **Senior Researcher (5+ years)**: Base salary GBP 125k-200k, total compensation up to GBP 600k.

**Key Trends**:

- **Strict non-compete clauses**: Some quant professionals face garden leave periods of up to three years, leading firms to use signing bonuses and guaranteed compensation to attract talent.
- **Sector divergence**: FX desks have performed strongly due to market volatility, while traditional investment banking teams have seen relatively subdued bonuses.

---

## 3. Asia-Pacific (APAC): A Growing Market with Strong Potential

The APAC market has grown steadily in recent years. Although overall compensation remains below Western levels, demand for top quant talent is robust.

### Hong Kong:

- **Junior Quant Researcher (PhD graduate)**: Base salary $90k-$150k, total compensation up to $240k.
- **Senior Researcher (10 years experience)**: Base salary $180k-$250k, total compensation including a 5%-20% PnL share.

### Singapore:

- **Quant Researcher (2-5 years experience)**: Base salary $90k-$110k, total compensation up to $180k.
- **Quant Trader (HFT)**: Base salary $150k-$170k, total compensation including a 10%-40% profit share.

**Key Trends**:

- **Sell-side to buy-side migration**: In recent years, sell-side professionals disappointed by bonuses have moved in large numbers to buy-side institutions such as hedge funds.
- **Emerging technology demand**: Candidates with AI and natural language processing (NLP) skills are highly sought after in the region.

---

## 4. China: A Rapidly Rising Force in Quantitative Finance

As China's financial markets continue to open, demand for quant talent has surged, particularly in hedge funds and high-frequency trading.

- **Junior Quant Researcher (PhD graduate)**: Base salary $60k-$200k, total compensation up to $300k.
- **Senior Prop Trader**: Base salary $100k-$170k, plus a 10%-35% profit share.
- **Senior Researcher (10+ years experience)**: Base salary $180k-$250k, total compensation up to $500k.

**Key Trends**:

- **Local and international talent valued equally**: Quant professionals with overseas experience are highly sought after, particularly for their global perspective and technical expertise.
